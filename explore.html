Trails & Hikes

Yellowstone offers over 900 miles of maintained trails ranging from accessible boardwalks to strenuous backcountry routes. The table below highlights a selection of popular hikes. Always check current trail conditions at a visitor center before heading out.

Trail Name Difficulty Distance Elevation Gain Highlights
Old Faithful Observation Point Easy 0.9 mi 177 ft Overhead view of Old Faithful eruptions
Mystic Falls Trail Easy 2.6 mi 300 ft Waterfall, bison meadows, geyser views
Grand Prismatic Overlook Easy 1.6 mi 165 ft Aerial view of the famous rainbow spring
Mount Washburn Moderate 6.4 mi 1,400 ft 360° panoramic summit views, bighorn sheep
Fairy Falls Trail Moderate 5.0 mi 200 ft Tallest accessible waterfall in the park (197 ft)
Avalanche Peak Strenuous 4.0 mi 2,100 ft Stunning alpine views of the Absaroka Range
Shoshone Lake via DeLacy Creek Moderate 6.0 mi 300 ft Largest backcountry lake in lower 48 states
Black Canyon of the Yellowstone Strenuous 19.0 mi 1,800 ft Remote canyon, river fishing, wildlife corridor

Wildlife Viewing

Yellowstone is one of the few places in the lower 48 states where large predators and their prey still interact in a nearly intact ecosystem. Early morning and dusk offer the best viewing opportunities.

  • 🦬 American Bison — over 4,000 in the park
  • 🐺 Gray Wolf — reintroduced in 1995
  • 🐻 Grizzly & Black Bear
  • 🦌 Elk — largest population in North America
  • 🦅 Bald Eagle & Osprey
  • 🐟 Cutthroat Trout
